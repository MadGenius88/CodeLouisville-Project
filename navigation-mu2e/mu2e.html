The Mu2e Project

Mu2e is a High Energy Physics experiment currently in development at the Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory (Fermilab), just outside Chicago, Illinois.

The experiment has a very specific goal in mind, which has governed all of the design considerations: to observe charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) in direct conversion of a muon to an electron. (Hence, "Mu2e".)

Lepton flavor violation has been observed in neutrino oscillations, and CLFV is also allowed in the current Standard Model (SM), although it is predicted to be heavily kinematically suppressed.

However, many theories of physics beyond the SM predict more frequent CLFV, like the direct conversion of a muon to an electron. At Mu2e's conclusion, we will (hopefully!) have either a more rigorous limit on CLFV's kinematic suppression, or a direct observation of CLFV suggesting physics beyond the Standard Model.

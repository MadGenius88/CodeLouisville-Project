The BaBar Project

BaBar is a High Energy Physics experiment located at SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, near Stanford University, in California.

The goal of the experiment is to study the violation of charge and parity (CP) symmetry in the decays of B mesons. This violation manifests itself as different behaviour between particles and anti-particles and is the first step to explain the absence of anti-particles in everyday life.

To study CP violation the BaBar experiment exploits the 9.1 GeV electron beam and the 3 GeV positron beam of the PEP-II accelerator. The two beams collide in the center of the experiment, producing Y(4S) mesons which decay into equal numbers of B and anti-B mesons.

Project description is courtesy of the BaBar public information page.

UofL's Inclusive Hadron Studies

With much of BaBar's initial CP-violation studies in completion, many research groups have turned to studying other HEP-related phenomena using BaBar's rich data set.

UofL's HEP group is currently working to compile a list of inclusive production measurements on several, relatively common hadrons produced at the BaBar detector. These studies focus on precise measurement of the production rate of the hadrons as a function of momentum, at the various event CoM-energies at which BaBar operated.
